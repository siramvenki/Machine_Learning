From: Ajit Singh, Director, eBusiness Consulting
Subject: Growing the new customer accounts

Team,

Carnegie Financial Services has retained us once again, with good news and bad news from their Sales department. The good news is that their new deep penetration sales initiative is succeeding! CFS has secured several new major accounts -- companies which previously purchased only from their competitors, including those old-time financial service bastions in Manhattan. The bad news is that these new customers are only purchasing one or two of their products, instead of the wide array of products they sell to their more established customers. In fact, revenue from their newly acquired customers is only about one-tenth that of their older wholesale customers. They're hoping that they can do better than that.

CFS is relying on us again to help grow these new accounts. In fact, their Senior VP of Marketing, Neil Arnot, and the VP of Marketing, Raymond Christianson, have already devised an approach to introduce selected products to each new customer. To do this, they need to know which products are most appropriate to sell to which new customers. After talking with their CIO, Dan Zeck, they think that their historical data includes sufficiently detailed information for this process. They discussed using a set of techniques called data mining to help ferret out the information they're looking for based on the buying habits of existing customers and their profiles. This has worked well for other companies, and I am confident that you can help them to implement it successfully at CFS. Neil has already retained the services of an expert in Data Mining to act as a consultant. But he is expensive and his time is limited, and he is also not as familiar with the wholesale food business. So it is up to you to get started, using his expertise and that of his assistants as you need it.

I have attached some documents which should be helpful in completing this task. The data for existing customers and new customers have been provided, which is created by the joint effort of CFS’s IT department and the expert consultant's team. The data explanation will help you to read through the data and figure out the meaning behind different attributes. The template should be used for your final report.

Thank you for your continued commitment,

Ajit Singh
Director, eBusiness Consulting
